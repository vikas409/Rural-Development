Who We Are

Rural Educational And Development Society is a non-profit organisation, established in 2002 that works primarily in the domain of Education, Health, Disaster Management, Art & Culture, Water, Agriculture, Minority, Gender, Housing, Child & Youth Development, Human Rights, Specially Abled, Right to Information & Advocacy.

Since its inception, READS has been actively involved in a variety of initiatives aimed at empowering marginalized communities. Through our programs, we have focused on improving access to quality education, raising awareness on health and sanitation, and promoting sustainable development practices Its primary office is in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh

Our team works in partnership with local communities to identify needs and implement impactful programs in education, healthcare, and economic development.

Vision

To create a healthier, more educated, and empowered rural society, where every individual has access to essential knowledge and resources for a better quality of life.

Mission

To improve the lives of underserved rural communities by promoting education, health awareness, and sustainable development, while empowering women, children, and marginalized groups to achieve a better future.

Our Programs

We run multiple initiatives focused on creating lasting change in the communities we serve.

Education for All

Providing quality education to underprivileged children through school construction, teacher training, and scholarship programs.
Our NGO also provides free study materials, mentorship, and digital classrooms to bridge the gap in remote villages.

Healthcare Access

The Medical Health Camp was organized to provide essential healthcare services to underserved communities, ensuring early diagnosis, treatment, and awareness about preventive healthcare
The camp offered free medical check-ups, consultations, and distribution of medicines to those in need. Qualified doctors and healthcare professionals conducted screenings for common illnesses, chronic diseases, and nutritional deficiencies. Awareness sessions were held on hygiene, sanitation, disease prevention, and the importance of regular health check-ups. Special focus was given to maternal and child health, elderly care, and lifestyle diseases such as diabetes and hypertension. Over 300 individuals benefited from the camp, receiving timely medical intervention and health guidance. Future plans include organizing regular health camps in rural and slum areas, collaborating with hospitals for specialized treatments, and launching mobile health units to extend healthcare accessibility to remote region

Rain Water Recharging Awareness Camp

The above was organized to educate communities about the importance of rainwater harvesting and groundwater conservation.
The initiative aimed to address water scarcity issues by promoting sustainable water management practices. Experts conducted awareness sessions on various rainwater harvesting techniques, including rooftop collection, recharge pits, and check dams, to help individuals and communities implement effective water conservation methods. Demonstrations were held to showcase simple and cost-effective ways to collect and store rainwater for household and agricultural use. Informational pamphlets were distributed, and interactive discussions encouraged community participation in local water conservation efforts. Many households and institutions adopted rainwater harvesting techniques as a result of the program. Future plans include expanding awareness campaigns, collaborating with government agencies for large-scale water conservation projects, and setting up model rainwater harvesting systems in schools and public places to serve as learning centers for sustainable water management
